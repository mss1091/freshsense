How It Works

From Gas Molecules to Your Phone

FreshSense transforms invisible biochemical signals into clear, actionable freshness intelligence. Here's every step of the journey — from your fridge shelf to your smartphone.

Step 01

Sense — Chemical Detection at the Source

The FreshSense hardware module sits directly on any refrigerator shelf — no modification to your appliance required. Inside the module, an array of Metal-Oxide Semiconductor (MOS) gas sensors continuously samples the air for Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) that food naturally emits as it decays.

GASES DETECTED

  • Ethylene (C₂H₄) — Emitted by ripening fruits and vegetables
  • Ammonia (NH₃) — Released during protein and amino acid breakdown
  • Hydrogen Sulfide (H₂S) — Indicator of egg and meat spoilage
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) — General metabolic decay marker

The sensor array reads gas concentrations in parts per million (ppm) every few minutes. An onboard ESP32 microcontroller preprocesses, timestamps, and packages the data locally before transmission — ensuring data integrity even during brief network outages.

Step 02

Transmit — Secure Cloud Communication

Sensor data is securely transmitted via your home Wi-Fi network to our cloud backend hosted on AWS and Google Cloud. All data is encrypted end-to-end — from the moment it leaves the ESP32 to the moment it reaches our servers.

The module's low-power design means it can run on USB-C power (connected to a small power bank or fridge-internal USB port) or battery power — no rewiring, no electrician, no hassle. Simply place it, plug it in, and go.

Step 03

Analyze — AI-Powered Freshness Intelligence

In the cloud, our ML pipeline processes incoming sensor data through a hybrid model architecture:

🌳 Random Forest

Pattern classification model that categorizes multi-gas signatures into three freshness states: Fresh, Caution, and Spoiled. Trained on controlled spoilage experiments across dozens of food types.

🔄 LSTM Network

Long Short-Term Memory neural network that captures temporal patterns in gas concentration changes — enabling accurate regression predictions of hours remaining until spoilage for each detected food category.

Together, these models don't just tell you what state your food is in — they predict the future, giving you actionable time to use, cook, or freeze items before they cross the freshness threshold.

Step 04

Act — Real-Time Insights in Your Pocket

The FreshSense mobile app (built with React Native for iOS and Android) surfaces all of this intelligence in a beautiful, intuitive dashboard:

  • Per-item freshness dashboard — See every monitored food item with its current state and countdown timer
  • Spoilage countdown timers — Know exactly how many hours of freshness remain
  • Push notifications — Get alerted before food transitions from Caution to Spoiled
  • AI recipe suggestions — Receive personalized recipes for items in Caution state so nothing goes to waste
